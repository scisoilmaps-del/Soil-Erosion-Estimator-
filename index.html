How to get percent slope
  1. Best source: use a conservation plan, RUSLE2 worksheet, NRCS planner value, LiDAR/topographic map, or Web Soil Survey slope class for the field.
  2. Field method: measure vertical rise over horizontal run, then calculate (rise ÷ run) × 100. Example: 6 ft rise over 100 ft run = 6% slope.
  3. Phone/map method: use a phone GPS/elevation app, laser level, transit, or two marked points with known elevation difference. Use the dominant field slope, not the steepest short spot.
