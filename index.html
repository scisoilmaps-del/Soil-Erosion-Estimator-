Slope length should be the representative overland flow path, not the whole field length. Stop at a terrace, grassed waterway, field border, concentrated flow channel, or depositional area.
How to choose slope length and percent slope
  1. Best source: use a conservation plan, RUSLE2 worksheet, NRCS planner value, LiDAR/topographic map, or Web Soil Survey slope class for the field.
  2. Slope length: measure the main overland flow path from where runoff begins to where deposition begins, runoff enters a grassed waterway, terrace channel, ditch, concentrated flow path, or field edge.
  3. Percent slope: use the dominant representative slope for the field or slope segment, not the steepest short spot. Calculate (rise ÷ run) × 100.
  4. Complex fields: split the field into slope segments and run each segment separately.
